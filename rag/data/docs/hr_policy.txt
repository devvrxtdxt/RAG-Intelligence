Human Resources Leave and Attendance Policy — Effective January 2024

1. Leave Entitlements
All full-time employees are entitled to the following annual leave:
- Casual Leave: 12 days per calendar year, non-cumulative. Casual leave may be taken in half-day increments with at least 24 hours prior notice to the reporting manager.
- Sick Leave: 10 days per calendar year. A medical certificate is required for absences exceeding two consecutive days. Unused sick leave may be carried forward up to a maximum of 20 days.
- Earned Leave (Privilege Leave): 15 days per calendar year, accrued at 1.25 days per month. Earned leave may be accumulated up to 45 days. Encashment of earned leave is permitted at separation, calculated at the last drawn basic salary.
- Maternity Leave: 26 weeks for the first two children, as per statutory requirements.
- Paternity Leave: 10 working days within one month of the child's birth.
- Bereavement Leave: 5 days for immediate family members.

2. Attendance and Working Hours
Standard working hours are 9:00 AM to 6:00 PM, Monday through Friday, with a one-hour lunch break. Employees must log attendance via the biometric system or the HR portal by 9:15 AM. Three instances of late arrival (after 9:30 AM) within a calendar month will result in a half-day casual leave deduction.

3. Work From Home Policy
Employees in eligible roles may work from home up to two days per week with prior manager approval. WFH requests must be submitted on the HR portal by 6:00 PM the previous working day. During WFH, employees must be available on Slack and respond within 15 minutes during core hours (10:00 AM – 4:00 PM). Managers may revoke WFH privileges if productivity benchmarks are not met for two consecutive review cycles.

4. Leave Application Procedure
All leave requests must be submitted through the HR portal. Casual leave requires approval from the immediate manager. Leave exceeding five consecutive days requires additional approval from the department head. Unapproved absences will be treated as loss of pay.

5. Holiday Calendar
The company observes 10 national and regional holidays per year. The holiday list is published on the HR portal by December 1 of the preceding year. Employees required to work on holidays will receive compensatory off within 30 days.
